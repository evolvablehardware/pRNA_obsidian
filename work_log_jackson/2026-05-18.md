- Ran evaluations of the circuit, batches of 50 with 10s between each batch to approximate evolution conditions. I ran this 20 times with 60s between each run. It appears that the 60s was not enough to fully reset the temperature, as the starting amount of pulses in each evolution simulation decreased over time until it eventually stabilized. 
	- It's interesting how the circuit evaluates to around 40k initially and drops off over time. I would except it to reach the 40k amount somewhere in between as this should be closer to the evolution conditions.
	- We should try using heatsinks at some point, this will improve consistency 
	- It appears while the pulses remain at a stable mean as the temperature increases, there is also a much greater volatility in that area. I think that it likely these conditions were more extreme than those encountered during evolution, so the circuit was not optimized for them. It's also possible that the higher temperature inherently brings more variance.
	- We could also start evaluating random circuits during mutation times instead of idling. This would keep the fpga temperature more stable. Currently the last uploaded circuit just remains on the chip, but since circuits are only evaluated for a few seconds the behavior afterwards is far from consistent. I think this would probably be done in the icefarm states; just upload random circuits during downtime. Need to take great care to not provide evolution advantages from these circuits through.
